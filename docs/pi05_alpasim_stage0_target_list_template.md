# PI0.5-to-Driving Target List Template

## Goal
Use this list to track researchers, technical angels, founder-advisors, and compute-credit programs who are good fits for a research-phase autonomy / embodied-AI project.

## How To Use
- Keep the list small and high signal.
- Prioritize people who can evaluate the technical question honestly.
- Track response quality, not just whether someone replies.
- The best validation question is:
  - “Is this research direction worth backing for the next 3-6 months?”

## Suggested Segments

### Researchers
Look for:
- embodied AI
- robotics manipulation + transfer learning
- autonomy simulation
- behavior cloning / closed-loop policy learning
- multimodal foundation models for control

### Technical Angels
Look for:
- ex-researcher operators
- robotics/autonomy angels
- technically deep founder-angels
- people comfortable funding work before product clarity

### Founder-Advisors
Look for:
- AV stack builders
- controls / planning people
- infra founders who understand simulator-to-policy loops
- people who can tell you whether your bottleneck diagnosis is sane

### Programs
Look for:
- cloud compute credits
- research-support incubators
- grants for frontier AI / robotics / autonomy
- programs that accept infrastructure-first technical work

## Questions To Ask
- Is the core research thesis worth pursuing?
- What one experiment would most strengthen the evidence?
- Does the current result look like undertraining, route leakage, or a deeper structural issue?
- Would you back the next 3-6 months of this work with time, compute, introductions, or capital?
